Tours & Field Trips

Educational and engaging experiences for groups of all ages

School Field Trips

The Duquesne Incline offers educational field trips for students of all ages. Our programs are designed to meet educational standards while providing a fun and memorable experience.

Educational Benefits

  • Learn about Pittsburgh's industrial history
  • Explore the engineering principles behind the incline system
  • Understand the importance of community preservation efforts
  • Experience one of Pittsburgh's most iconic landmarks

Program Details

Our standard school field trip includes:

  • Round-trip ride on the incline
  • Guided tour of the upper station and machine room
  • Interactive presentation on the history and mechanics of the incline
  • Special viewing area for Pittsburgh's panoramic views
  • Educational materials for teachers (upon request)

Ready to Book?

Field trips should be scheduled at least 3 weeks in advance. Group discounts are available for 20 or more students.

Tour Options

Standard Tour

Duration: 45 minutes

Price: $8 per person (minimum 10 people)

Our standard tour includes a round-trip ride on the incline, a guided tour of the upper station, and time to enjoy the view and visit the gift shop.

Engineering Tour

Duration: 60 minutes

Price: $12 per person (minimum 10 people)

Perfect for those interested in how things work! Get an in-depth look at the historical machinery that powers the incline, with detailed explanations of the mechanical systems.

Historical Tour

Duration: 75 minutes

Price: $15 per person (minimum 10 people)

Dive into Pittsburgh's rich industrial history with our historical tour. Learn about the city's development and how the incline played a crucial role in its growth.

Photography Tour

Duration: 90 minutes

Price: $18 per person (minimum 8 people)

Capture stunning views of Pittsburgh with our photography tour. Includes extended time at our best vantage points and photography tips from our knowledgeable guides.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the maximum group size?

+

We can accommodate groups of up to 50 people. Larger groups will be divided into smaller tours that run consecutively.

Are tours accessible for people with disabilities?

+

While the historical nature of our facility presents some accessibility challenges, we strive to accommodate all visitors. Please contact us in advance to discuss specific accommodations for your group.

Do you offer educational materials for teachers?

+

Yes, we provide pre-visit and post-visit educational materials for teachers upon request. These materials align with Pennsylvania educational standards and cover topics in history, engineering, and local heritage.

Can we bring our own lunch?

+

There are no picnic facilities at the Duquesne Incline. However, there are several nearby parks with picnic areas, and we can provide recommendations for nearby restaurants that accommodate groups.

What payment methods do you accept for group bookings?

+

For group bookings, we accept school purchase orders, checks, and cash. Please note that we require a 50% deposit to confirm your booking, with the balance due on the day of your visit.
