What to See & Do

Image coutsey of TheQuirkyTravelGuy

The Historic Ride

Experience a piece of living history on our meticulously preserved incline cars. The Duquesne Incline has been in continuous operation since 1877, making it one of the oldest funicular railways in the country.

During your ride, enjoy the gradual ascent up Mount Washington, with increasingly spectacular views of Pittsburgh's skyline and three rivers.

Image coutsey of PlaceWise

Observation Deck

The upper station features an observation deck that offers what USA Today called "one of the most beautiful views in America." From this vantage point, you can see the entire downtown Pittsburgh skyline, the confluence of the three rivers, and the rolling hills beyond.

The observation deck is accessible with your ticket and is a perfect spot for photography, especially at sunset or after dark when the city lights create a magical panorama.

Image coutsey of NoHomeJustRoam

Museum & Exhibits

The upper station houses a small museum featuring historical photographs and mechanical displays that showcase the incline's rich history and engineering. Learn about Pittsburgh's industrial past and how the incline played a vital role in the city's development.

Don't miss the viewing window where you can observe the original hoisting machinery from 1877 that still powers the incline today!

Getting Here

The lower station of the Duquesne Incline is located on West Carson Street at the base of Mount Washington. The upper station is at 1220 Grandview Avenue.

Parking

Limited paid parking is available at the Lower Station on West Carson Street. Street parking is also available near the Upper Station on Grandview Avenue.

Public Transportation

The Duquesne Incline is accessible via Port Authority bus routes. The nearest bus stops are served by routes 40 and 44.
