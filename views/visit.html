What to See & Do

The Historic Ride

Experience a piece of living history on our meticulously preserved incline cars. The Duquesne Incline has been in continuous operation since 1877, making it one of the oldest funicular railways in the country.

During your ride, enjoy the gradual ascent up Mount Washington, with increasingly spectacular views of Pittsburgh's skyline and three rivers.

Observation Deck

The upper station features an observation deck that offers what USA Today called "one of the most beautiful views in America." From this vantage point, you can see the entire downtown Pittsburgh skyline, the confluence of the three rivers, and the rolling hills beyond.

The observation deck is accessible with your ticket and is a perfect spot for photography, especially at sunset or after dark when the city lights create a magical panorama.

Museum & Exhibits

The upper station houses a small museum featuring historical photographs and mechanical displays that showcase the incline's rich history and engineering. Learn about Pittsburgh's industrial past and how the incline played a vital role in the city's development.

Don't miss the viewing window where you can observe the original hoisting machinery from 1877 that still powers the incline today!

Getting Here

The lower station of the Duquesne Incline is located on West Carson Street at the base of Mount Washington. The upper station is at 1220 Grandview Avenue.

Parking

Limited paid parking is available at the Lower Station on West Carson Street. Street parking is also available near the Upper Station on Grandview Avenue.

Public Transportation

The Duquesne Incline is accessible via Port Authority bus routes. The nearest bus stops are served by routes 40 and 44.

Accessibility Information

The Duquesne Incline is a historic facility that predates modern accessibility standards. We strive to accommodate all visitors, but there are some limitations due to the historical nature of our facility.

Mobility Access

  • The Lower Station has a ramp entrance, but assistance may be required.
  • The incline cars can accommodate standard wheelchairs, but space is limited.
  • The Upper Station has several steps and no elevator. Staff members are available to assist visitors when possible.
  • The observation deck is accessible via several steps from the Upper Station.

Service Animals

Service animals are welcome at the Duquesne Incline. Please inform staff when you arrive with a service animal.

Additional Assistance

If you require special accommodations, please contact us in advance at (412) 381-1665, and we will do our best to assist you.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is the incline ride?

+

The ride on the Duquesne Incline takes approximately 2-3 minutes each way. Many visitors ride up, spend time at the observation deck and museum, then ride back down.

Are reservations required?

+

No, reservations are not required for individuals or small groups. Tickets are sold on a first-come, first-served basis. However, for groups of 10 or more, we recommend contacting us in advance.

Is there a restaurant at the Duquesne Incline?

+

The Duquesne Incline does not have an on-site restaurant. However, there are several restaurants along Grandview Avenue near the Upper Station that offer dining with spectacular views of the city.

Can I bring my pet on the incline?

+

Service animals are welcome on the Duquesne Incline. However, we cannot accommodate pets that are not service animals due to space limitations and safety considerations.

Is photography allowed?

+

Yes, photography for personal use is permitted and encouraged at the Duquesne Incline. The observation deck offers prime photo opportunities of Pittsburgh's skyline. For commercial photography, please contact us in advance.
