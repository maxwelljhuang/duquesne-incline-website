A Pittsburgh Landmark Since 1877

The Duquesne Incline is a century-old cable car system that has served as both a practical transportation method and a beloved tourist attraction.

Originally built to transport workers from the riverfront to the communities on top of the steep slope, it now offers visitors stunning views of Pittsburgh's three rivers and skyline.

The upper station houses a museum featuring historic photographs and mechanical displays that showcase the incline's rich history and engineering marvel.
